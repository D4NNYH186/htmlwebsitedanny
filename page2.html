Unit 1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business.

There are various aspects of IT incorporated within business models in the present day and IT has become a vital part of every business plan regardless of how small or large the company is.
In this section I will discuss some of the different ways and reasons IT is implemented in the present day marketplace.[1]
One of the more widely used aspects of IT used across businesses is email and has been for a long time, this is used in a variety of ways, whether it be communication between employees, with customers, suppliers and even other associates linked with the company. As time has progressed, so have the options for IT communication, various tools have evolved and are now becoming a lot more advanced, VOIP (Voice over Internet protocol)/IP telephony Is one of these, this allows people to make telephone calls at any time providing there is a solid connection to the internet,
VOIP converts a users voice into a digital signal which is then sent to the other user via the internet. Another commonly used tool is live chat systems (Zendesk, Drift),
online meeting tools and video-conferencing systems (Nasdaq meeting portal, Zoom, lifeWire) are now a key part of the operation of a business also, being used to conference with teams and management not based in the same premises.

Moving away from communication tools we can now take a look at other aspects of IT and tools used in business looking at various management systems whether this be IMS (Inventory Management Systems) used to manage and order stock, also having these connected to a POS (point of sale) system, this ensures that when an item is sold, this is reflected in the stock levels and will no longer be counted in the inventory, DMS (Data Management Systems) that store data on servers and storage devices,
that along with the introduction of ‘the cloud’ that allows users to access their data anywhere on the planet, providing they have internet access, this alleviates the use for filing cabinets and paper filing systems, MIS (Management Information Systems) that can manage employee productivity, expenses, identify areas of improvement and profitability. CRM (Customer Relationship Management) this is a way for businesses to track and manage correspondence from customers, whether it be storing emails and scanned documents to creating call logs when customers have contacted via phone. Unit 1.2 Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully

Within the industry in 2019, there are a variety of skills, methods and resources that can be utilised to help complete a digital project. I would like to discuss some of these, to start I would like to touch on the kind of skills that are needed to help manage a digital project, these are 4 skills that are prime in the success of any project. Firstly, communication and collaboration. The ability to communicate well, to understand and be understood is crucial to the effectiveness of any team members working on the project.
If the member cannot communicate with their manager, client or team, wires will be crossed, confusion will arise which can only lead to problems for the project. [1] The next skill I would like to focus on is Time management, this is essential to completing any project, If you cannot prioritise tasks and manage your time to fulfil these, then chances are your project will struggle, always be prepared and allow time for mistakes and problems, these will occur and if you do not prepare yourself for this, then when a problem does occur other tasks will fall behind, delaying the project and missing deadlines, overall decreasing productivity. Another crucial skill to completing a successful project is problem solving, locating problems, understanding them and devising a plan or idea on how to fix it.
Finally I would like to talk about another skill, that if not utilised will seriously damage your project, subject and product knowledge, the tech industry is consistently changing, new dev languages, gadgets, gizmos and engineering are frequently being released and updated. To be a successful developer you need to try and keep up to date with the skill set you have and would like to maintain, this doesn’t mean learning everything, but tailoring your knowledge to match the skill set needed for the project in hand. I would like to add knowing the designated brief for the project is also vital in the success for a project, if the dev does not know or understand what is needed, then how can this be delivered successfully? It cannot and therefore could derail the project.

Taking a look at methodology there seems to be two popular methods used, Agile and waterfall, ‘Agile is a much more evolved methodology, built on the backbone of flexibility and collaboration. While waterfall has a strict plan to follow, agile approaches give teams the ability to respond to change quickly and efficiently. This also allows the client or stakeholder to come in at different stages and make sure their expectations are being met before moving forward.’[2] There are also a variety of tools that can be utilised for the successful completion of a digital project. With the use of coding software like VS code and node.js developers can write simple code to advanced code using a variety of languages (html., JavaScript, iOs) this teamed with the use of websites like ‘Trello’ to help task management, this can be used by the entire team and helps the team on a whole see at what point each developer is at, ‘Slack’ a sharing/messaging site that has a similar layout and vaguely resembles facebook, this can be used to communicate and share ideas between team members and ‘GitHub’, which is ‘a development platform inspired by the way you work. From open source to business, you can host and review code, manage projects, and build software alongside’[3] other developers, this is a great platform to save and share your work, whether this be for personal gratification, or to share with a colleague.
